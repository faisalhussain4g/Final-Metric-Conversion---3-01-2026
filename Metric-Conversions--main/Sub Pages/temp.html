TEMPERATURE CONVERSION

Temperature conversion is the process of changing a temperature value from one unit of measurement to another while keeping the actual level of heat or cold the same. It is commonly used to convert between temperature scales such as Celsius (°C), Fahrenheit (°F), and Kelvin (K). Temperature conversion helps people understand and compare temperature readings used in different countries, scientific studies, weather reports, and daily life. Each temperature scale is based on different reference points. Celsius is based on the freezing and boiling points of water, Fahrenheit is commonly used for weather in some countries, and Kelvin is mainly used in scientific calculations. Because these scales do not increase in the same way, temperature conversion often requires specific formulas rather than simple multiplication or division. Temperature conversion is important in science, medicine, cooking, meteorology, and engineering. By converting temperatures accurately, information can be shared clearly and safely across different fields and regions, ensuring correct measurements and better understanding of environmental and scientific conditions.



Fahrenheit to Kelvin Conversion

This type converts temperature from Fahrenheit (°F) directly to Kelvin (K).
Examples:
32°F = 273 K

Celsius to Fahrenheit Conversion

This type converts temperature from Celsius (°C) to Fahrenheit (°F).
Examples:
0°C = 32°F
25°C = 77°F

Fahrenheit to Celsius Conversion

This type converts temperature from Fahrenheit (°F) to Celsius (°C).
Examples:
32°F = 0°C
68°F = 20°C

Celsius to Kelvin Conversion

This type converts temperature from Celsius (°C) to Kelvin (K).
Examples:
0°C = 273 K
100°C = 373 K

Kelvin to Celsius Conversion

This type converts temperature from Kelvin (K) to Celsius (°C).
Examples:
273 K = 0°C
300 K = 27°C

Kelvin to Fahrenheit Conversion

This type converts temperature from Kelvin (K) to Fahrenheit (°F).
Examples:
273 K = 32°F
